Mac Holds the Top Spot After a Fierce Wing Day Showdown

Wing Day delivered exactly what students had been waiting for. Across a packed afternoon on Garcia Field, the four wings — Clark, Fell, Mac, and Tupper — went head-to-head in a series of events including Wet Tug-of-War, soccer, spikeball, and Ultimate Reaction Time. The energy was high, the competition higher, and by the end of the day the standings had shifted in ways nobody quite expected.

Mac retained their first-place position, a result that comes as little surprise given their commanding lead in the Learning Commons Book Challenge — an ongoing points event that has proven to be one of the biggest differentiators in the overall standings. Meanwhile, both Fell and Tupper had strong showings on the field, with each wing leaping Clark to climb the rankings. Clark, for now, sits at the bottom of the table — but with Wing Day points still being tallied, nothing is set in stone.

SGS Celebrates Asian Heritage Month

This week SGS marked Asian Heritage Month with two standout moments. A Filipino Fusion food truck rolled onto campus Tuesday at lunch, and a special assembly presentation brought the community together to reflect on the richness of Asian cultures. The highlight? A Hot Ones-inspired spicy food challenge featuring university counsellor Ms. Shrestha and student Herbert Zhu — who powered through increasingly fiery bites while sharing how food builds connection, carries identity, and shapes the cultures we come from.

Hantavirus Outbreak on Cruise Ship Leaves Passengers in Critical Condition

A rare hantavirus outbreak aboard a cruise ship has now reached 11 confirmed cases, with a French woman critically ill on an artificial lung in Paris. Four Canadians from the ship are currently isolating on Vancouver Island — making this one uncomfortably close to home.

B.C. Nurses Vote 98% in Favour of Strike — Right Before the World Cup

Nearly all of B.C.'s 55,000 nurses have voted to strike after six months of stalled contract talks over pay, benefits, and staffing shortages. With the FIFA World Cup kicking off in Vancouver next month, the union's president says "the whole world is watching us."
